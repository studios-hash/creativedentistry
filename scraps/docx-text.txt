Overview
Eyebrow  —  Overview
Heading  —  Prevention is the most powerful dentistry there is
Lead paragraph  —  Most of what threatens your smile — decay, gum disease, a small crack — is far easier and far gentler to address when we catch it early. That is the entire point of a regular visit here.
Body paragraph  —  Every six months we take a complete look: a thorough exam, a professional cleaning, digital X-rays only when they are warranted, and an honest conversation about anything we see. No upsell, no scare tactics — just a clear picture of where you stand.
What's Included
Comprehensive exams — a full evaluation of teeth, gums, bite, and soft tissue.
Professional cleanings — gentle, thorough hygiene that removes what brushing cannot.
Digital X-rays — low-radiation imaging, taken only when clinically useful.
Oral-cancer screening — a quick, painless check at every exam.
Gum health — periodontal evaluation to keep your foundation strong.
Sealants &amp; fluoride — simple protection for vulnerable teeth.
What to Expect
Eyebrow  —  What to Expect
Heading  —  An unhurried visit, start to finish
1.  Settle in  —  Coffee or tea in hand, we review any changes to your health and listen to your concerns.
2.  A complete look  —  Exam, imaging when needed, and a gentle cleaning from a hygienist who takes their time.
3.  On the same page  —  We walk through what we found together on screen — you leave knowing exactly where things stand.
Feature Block
Eyebrow  —  Why It Matters
Heading  —  Care that prevents, not just repairs
Body  —  A steady rhythm of preventive visits is the single best investment you can make in your smile — it keeps small things small, and keeps you out of the chair for the big ones.
FAQ
Q  How often should I come in?
A  For most patients, every six months. If you are managing gum disease or other concerns, we may suggest a tailored interval.
Q  Do you take X-rays every visit?
A  No. We take digital X-rays only when there is a clinical reason — and they are low-radiation when we do.
Q  I have not been to a dentist in years. Is that a problem?
A  Not at all, and you will not hear a word of judgment. We simply start with a complete picture and build a plan from there.
